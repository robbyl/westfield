Reverse Logistics









Reverse Logistics is the process of planning, implementing and controlling the efficient, cost effective recovery of equipment and the process of removing goods from their operating environment, is a function that companies need to take seriously if they wish to capture any value and properly dispose of their surplus equipment.

One consequence of delaying the decision to start implementing a reverse logistics programme to dispose of unwanted equipment is that any re-sale value could have decreased to the point of scrap value by the time the need to recover space or power becomes a necessity. Despite the general belief that there is a lot of money in scrap, it is only a fraction of what products can achieve in the resale marketplace.

Greenwoods Communications can provide decommissioning, deinstallation, transportation, auditing, storing, reselling and recycling services for communications equipment, offering our customers the opportunity to capture value and properly dispose of all redundant material. Our telecom recovery business manages projects for major global operators, OEMs, other licensed operators, enterprise customers and leading industry suppliers.
